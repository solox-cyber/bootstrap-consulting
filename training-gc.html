With Digital Transformation initiatives being accelerated since the Covid19 Pandemic started, Companies are now more exposed to various risks to include Information Security risks, operational risks, third-party risks, compliance management risks, and the list goes on. A recent survey of 100 digital and analytics transformation leaders from companies across industries and around the globe, carried out by Mckinsey & Company also goes to show this. The results revealed that the COVID-19 pandemic environment has heightened the disparity between risk management demands and existing capabilities. Essentially, there are skills-shortages.

GRC, which is fast becoming a hot career option, provides a good opportunity for IT Security Professionals looking to get into management and advisory roles within businesses. It also expands career options for Lawyers, Auditors and Risk Managers, amongst others.

Who should attend:

Business Leaders, Board Members, Project Managers, IT Business Analysts, Lawyers, Auditors, Risk Managers, and Intending GRC Professionals.

Duration: 20 Hours in all (4 hours per day).

Start Date: 5th March, 2022.

Start Time: 4.00pm GMT+1 (Saturdays and Sundays).

(Schedule can be adjusted to suit participants who are combining this with work, if the majority fall in this category).

Fee: $450 (or N200, 000) Financing Option

Course Outline:

  • Introduction to Governance, Risk, and Compliance: Understanding the key functions of each of the GRC roles, Understanding the relationship between GRC roles, Understanding the importance of GRC roles in achieving an organization's cyber security objectives and business goals Understanding the standard and regulatory environment (PCI DSS, SOC 1 & 2, ISO 27001, GDPR etc.), Understanding organizational culture as it relates to performance, risk, and compliance.
  • Governance: Understanding the role of governance professionals to align business and security strategy, Understanding what is expected out of governance role, Defining and developing organization policies and procedures, Setting out Key Performance Indicators. (Workshop on Developing and Reviewing Security Policy).
  • Risk: Understanding how organizations measure cyber security risk, Risk Framework and Process, Understanding a Risk Management Approach, Risk Identification, Risk Assessment & Analysis, Risk Evaluation and Treatment, Risk Reporting and Dashboards, 3rd Party Risk Management. (Workshop on Developing and Maintaining Risk Register).
  • Compliance: Understanding different compliance requirements for organizations, Understanding how to develop and maintain compliance management program, Understanding GAP assessment and organization maturity models, Conducting and managing internal and external audits, Compliance Implementation Approaches. (Workshop on Performing GAP Assessment/ Internal Audit).
